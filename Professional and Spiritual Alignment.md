### Professional and Spiritual Alignment

Throughout my educational journey, I have come to realize that my spiritual beliefs and professional development are closely connected. While studying Information Technology has helped me develop technical knowledge and problem-solving skills, my faith in Jesus Christ has shaped the kind of professional I aspire to become. Rather than viewing my career and my beliefs as separate parts of my life, I see them as complementary. My faith provides the values that guide my decisions, while my education gives me the tools to serve others in meaningful ways.

One of the most important principles that guides my life is faith in Jesus Christ. Technology is constantly changing, and there are many moments when learning new concepts can feel overwhelming. Throughout my coursework, I have completed projects involving web development, Python programming, virtualization, networking, and cybersecurity. Many of these assignments challenged me to step outside my comfort zone, but my faith reminded me that growth comes through patience, effort, and trusting in the Lord. Instead of becoming discouraged by difficult problems, I learned to approach them with perseverance and confidence that I could continue improving.

Another principle that strongly influences my professional goals is integrity. Information Technology professionals are entrusted with sensitive information, computer systems, and digital resources that require ethical judgment. Whether developing software, administering networks, or working in cybersecurity, honesty and responsibility are essential. I want to be someone who protects people's privacy, respects confidentiality, and uses technical knowledge only for ethical purposes. My studies have reinforced that technical ability without integrity can harm individuals and organizations, while integrity builds trust and strengthens professional relationships.

Finally, service is a principle that connects both my spiritual life and my career goals. I believe technology should improve people's lives and make information more accessible. My experiences creating websites, developing software, and studying cybersecurity have shown me that technology is ultimately about helping people solve problems. This principle is also reflected in my current service as a leader for the schedule and activities of the CJAS conference in Brazil. Planning and serving others has taught me the value of teamwork, organization, patience, and compassion. These same qualities are important in the workplace, where collaboration and a desire to help others contribute to successful projects and positive professional relationships.

As I continue my education in Information Technology, I hope to combine technical excellence with Christlike attributes. I want to be known not only for my skills but also for my honesty, kindness, reliability, and willingness to serve. My goal is to build a career where my professional decisions reflect my spiritual values and where I can use my knowledge to make a positive difference in the lives of others.
