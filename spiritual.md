# Spiritual Portfolio

## My Guiding Principles

My spiritual growth has influenced both my personal life and my professional goals. The following guiding principles help me make decisions, strengthen my faith, and remind me of the kind of person and IT professional I want to become.

---

## 1. Faith in Jesus Christ

Faith in Jesus Christ is the foundation of my life. It helps me trust Heavenly Father even when I do not know what the future will bring. As I continue my education in Information Technology, I know there will be challenges and opportunities to grow. My faith reminds me that if I seek the Lord's guidance, work hard, and remain faithful, He will direct my path and help me accomplish His purposes.

### Supporting Materials

### Proverbs 3:5–6

> "Trust in the Lord with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding."

This scripture reminds me that I do not need to have every answer today. As I continue learning and preparing for my career, I trust that the Lord will guide me one step at a time.

---

## 2. Integrity

Integrity means choosing to do what is right, even when no one is watching. In Information Technology, professionals often have access to confidential information and important systems. Because of this responsibility, I want to always act honestly, respect privacy, and make ethical decisions. Building trust is just as important as building technical skills.

### Supporting Materials

### Doctrine and Covenants 58:27

> "Men should be anxiously engaged in a good cause."

This scripture reminds me to take initiative and use my knowledge to make a positive difference while always acting honestly and responsibly.

### Personal Insight

**Integrity matters even when no one is watching.**

I want this reminder to guide my decisions throughout my career.

---

## 3. Service

Technology is about helping people. Whether I am solving technical problems, improving accessibility, or supporting coworkers, I want my work to reflect Christlike service. Serving others helps me remember that my career can bless the lives of many people.

### Supporting Materials

### Mosiah 2:17

> "When ye are in the service of your fellow beings ye are only in the service of your God."

This scripture reminds me that every opportunity to help someone is also an opportunity to serve God.

### John 13:35

> "By this shall all men know that ye are my disciples, if ye have love one to another."

Kindness, patience, and respect should always influence the way I work with others.

---

### Favorite Scripture
2 Nephi 9:18

> "But, behold, the righteous, the saints of the Holy One of Israel, they who have believed in the Holy One of Israel, they who have endured the crosses of the world, and despised the shame of it, they shall inherit the kingdom of God, which was prepared for them from the foundation of the world, and their joy shall be full forever."

This is my favorite scripture because it reminds me that discipleship is not always easy, but every challenge we faithfully endure has an eternal purpose. Throughout life, there will be moments of uncertainty, sacrifice, and difficult decisions, but this verse gives me hope that as I continue to trust in Jesus Christ and remain faithful, my efforts are never wasted.

As I pursue my education in Information Technology and prepare for my future career, I know there will be obstacles that require perseverance and faith. This scripture encourages me to keep moving forward with confidence, remembering that true joy comes from following the Savior rather than seeking temporary success. It also reminds me that my professional goals should never be separated from my spiritual values. I want to build a career founded on integrity, service, and faith, trusting that the Lord will guide my path as I strive to honor my covenants and bless the lives of others.

---

### Doctrine and Covenants 58:26–28

> "For behold, it is not meet that I should command in all things; for he that is compelled in all things, the same is a slothful and not a wise servant; wherefore he receiveth no reward."

> "Verily I say, men should be anxiously engaged in a good cause, and do many things of their own free will, and bring to pass much righteousness;"

> "For the power is in them, wherein they are agents unto themselves. And inasmuch as men do good they shall in nowise lose their reward."

This scripture reminds me that God expects me to use my agency with purpose and to take initiative in doing good rather than waiting to be told what to do. It has become especially meaningful to me this week as I reflected on my temple recommend interviews and my opportunity to serve as the leader for the schedule and activities of the CJAS conference in Brazil.

Through these experiences, I have come to better understand what it means to be "anxiously engaged in a good cause." Serving others requires willingness, preparation, and faith, and I have seen how the Lord blesses our efforts when we choose to act with a willing heart. This scripture also inspires me as I pursue a career in Information Technology. I want to be someone who takes initiative, solves problems, acts ethically, and uses my knowledge and skills to make a positive difference in the lives of others.

### Recent Spiritual Reflection

This week gave me a meaningful opportunity to reflect on my testimony and spiritual growth as I renewed my temple recommend. I first met with my bishop and later had an interview with a member of the stake presidency. These experiences encouraged me to thoughtfully consider the temple recommend questions and evaluate how I am living my faith each day. Rather than simply answering the questions, I found myself reflecting on my relationship with Jesus Christ, the covenants I have made, and the person I am striving to become.

During this same week, I also studied Doctrine and Covenants 58:26–28, which teaches that we should be "anxiously engaged in a good cause." This scripture reminded me that God trusts us to use our agency to act with faith, initiative, and purpose. It inspired me to recognize that meaningful growth often comes from choosing to serve and improve without waiting to be asked.

One way I have experienced this principle is through my service on the planning committee for the YSA conference in Brazil. As the leader responsible for the schedule and activities, I have learned that serving others requires preparation, teamwork, patience, and love. Although it can be challenging at times, this opportunity has strengthened my testimony that when we willingly dedicate our time and talents to a righteous cause, the Lord magnifies our efforts.

These experiences have reminded me that spiritual growth is an ongoing process. As I continue pursuing my education and future career in Information Technology, I hope to approach both my professional responsibilities and my opportunities to serve with faith, integrity, and a willing heart.

---

## My Growth

As I continue learning and growing, I have come to realize that spiritual and professional development go hand in hand. My education in Information Technology has strengthened my problem-solving skills, while my faith has taught me to approach challenges with patience, integrity, and hope. I want to become an IT professional who is known not only for technical knowledge but also for honesty, compassion, and a commitment to serving others.

My goal is to continue developing the skills and character necessary to make a positive difference wherever I work. I believe that as I continue to strengthen my testimony of Jesus Christ and follow His example, I will be better prepared to use my education and abilities to bless the lives of others.
